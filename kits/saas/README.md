# SaaS Landing

A conversion-first design system for modern software products. The look is
clean and trustworthy: near-black type on a near-white canvas, generous
whitespace, a medium-radius card language, and a single confident indigo
accent (`#5B5BD6`) that earns attention because it is used sparingly. Light
mode is the default — a matching dark scheme ships for hosts that request
it — and the type scale stays tight and legible rather than editorial, so
the page reads as a product, not a brochure.

This is a marketing page system, not a presentation deck. Every section is a
fluid, full-width band that a downstream caller fills through props: a sticky
navbar with in-page anchor links, a benefit-led hero, social proof, a
feature grid, a customer voice, transparent pricing, a closing call to
action, and a structured footer. Motion is deliberately restrained —
tasteful scroll-in reveals and gentle staggers — because the goal is to move
a visitor toward a single conversion, not to dazzle them.

## Composition

The chain mirrors how a high-converting landing page builds trust and pushes
toward signup. **Navbar** anchors the page and keeps the primary CTA in
reach at all times; its links jump to the section ids below. **Hero** states
the core promise above the fold with a primary and secondary action and an
optional product screenshot, so the value proposition lands before anything
else. **LogoCloud** immediately follows with low-commitment social proof —
recognizable customer wordmarks reassure the skeptic before they read on.

With trust seeded, **FeatureGrid** does the explaining: an icon-led 2- or
3-column grid that breaks the product into scannable benefits. **Testimonial**
then converts explanation into belief with a single human voice — one strong
quote carries more weight than a wall of features. **Pricing** arrives once
the visitor is convinced, presenting transparent tiers with one plan
highlighted to guide the choice. **CTA** is the closing push: a focused band
that restates the offer with nothing to distract from the button. **Footer**
closes the page with navigation, legal, and the wordmark — the calm landing
after the pitch. The rhythm alternates dense and sparse — hero, thin logo
strip, dense grid, intimate quote, structured pricing, single-message CTA —
so the eye keeps moving toward the conversion.
