# Restaurant

A warm, moody design system for fine dining. The look is candle-lit and
considered: a deep espresso canvas, warm cream type, and a single refined gold
accent (`#C8A24A`) used the way a restaurant uses brass — sparingly, on the
details that matter. Headlines are set in a Playfair Display serif for an
editorial, menu-card feel, while body copy stays in clean Inter so descriptions
and hours read plainly. This is the collection's only warm-dark system, and it
commits to that mood: every surface sits on the dark ground, imagery carries the
warmth, and the gold does the rest.

It is a complete restaurant page, not a marketing funnel. Each section is a
fluid, full-width band a downstream caller fills through props: a translucent
sticky navbar with a reserve action, a full-bleed atmospheric hero, the menu as
the centerpiece, a chef-and-philosophy image-text, a dish-and-room gallery,
practical hours and location, a closing reservation invitation, and a quiet
footer. Motion is restrained — gentle scroll-in reveals and staggered menu
items — so the page feels poised rather than busy. Use it for restaurants,
tasting-menu pages, wine bars, and intimate supper-club sites; reach for a
lighter, product-led system for anything that needs to read crisp and neutral.

## Composition

The chain walks a guest the way an evening unfolds — arrival, anticipation, the
meal, the story behind it, and the booking. **Navbar** anchors the page and
keeps "Reserve" within reach at every scroll position; its links jump to the
section ids below. **Hero** sets the mood before a single word of copy: a
full-bleed, dimly-lit image, the restaurant name in large serif, and one
invitation to reserve — atmosphere first, because dining is sold on feeling.

With the room established, **MenuList** is the centerpiece and the densest
beat: categories of dishes with price-aligned dotted leaders, the one section a
hungry visitor actually came for, so it lands early while attention is high.
**Story** then slows the pace — a single portrait beside a column of prose
gives the food provenance and a human voice, the emotional counterweight to the
menu's list. **Gallery** follows as a purely visual interlude, letting plating
and the dining room speak without copy and resetting the eye after reading.

**Hours** turns intent into logistics: opening times, address, and a phone
link, the practical answer to "can I actually go?" once the visitor is sold.
**Reservation** is the closing push — a full-bleed image and one focused
invitation to book, distraction-free by design. **Footer** lands the page
quietly with the wordmark, address, a hours summary, and social links. The
rhythm deliberately alternates expansive and intimate — full-bleed hero, dense
menu, quiet story, image grid, tidy info cards, full-bleed CTA — so the scroll
breathes like a meal paced across courses.
