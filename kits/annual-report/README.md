# Annual Report

A refined, authoritative design system for a company's yearly report — a
scroll-driven document that reports results to stakeholders with the gravitas of
print. The look is light and serious: a crisp neutral-paper canvas, ink-dark
text, and a single sober deep-navy accent (`#1E3A5F`). A serif display face
(Source Serif 4) carries the headlines and the oversized figures that are the
report's signature, paired with a clean sans (Inter) for body copy and a mono
(JetBrains Mono) for tabular figures, captions, and the wide-tracked kicker
labels. Corners are a measured medium radius. The system is single-scheme by
design — there is no dark variant — because a report wants the steady, even feel
of a printed page.

This is a data-rich presentation, not a magazine or a pitch deck. Every section
is a fluid, full-width beat a downstream caller fills through props: a serif
title page, a letter from leadership, animated key figures, financial charts,
a segment breakdown, a year-in-review timeline, the forward-looking outlook, and
a closing sign-off. Oversized numbers and themed charts carry the load — key
figures count up as they scroll into view, and the chart block slots render
static, token-themed SVG that re-skins with the system. Motion is restrained and
documentary: scroll-in reveals, staggered lists, a gentle cover parallax, and
count-ups that make the numbers land without theatrics.

## Composition

The chain reads the way an annual report reads — it earns trust before it asks
for it, and it moves from voice, to numbers, to narrative, to intent. **Cover**
opens like a title page: company name, report title, fiscal year, and a single
tagline over an optional full-bleed image, establishing the year's tone in one
serif statement. **LetterFromLeadership** follows with a human voice — the CEO's
address — so the reader meets a person before a spreadsheet; it sets the
interpretive frame for everything that follows.

With tone established, the report turns to evidence. **Highlights** delivers the
headline figures as oversized numbers that count up on scroll — the single most
important beat, and the reason a stakeholder opened the document. **FinancialChart**
then substantiates those figures with themed charts: revenue by year, a margin
trend, the shape of the cash flows. **SegmentBreakdown** decomposes the
consolidated result into where it actually came from, so the growth stops being
abstract. This is the dense, data-led core of the report, deliberately placed
after the warm-up and before the narrative cool-down.

The closing movement turns record into plan. **Milestones** gives the year a
narrative arc — the moments that defined it — pacing the reader down from the
charts. **Outlook** is the pivot to the future on a full navy field: the
strategy and numbered priorities for the year ahead, the report's only inverted
beat so the change of intent is felt as a change of color. **Closing** lands the
document quietly — a thank-you, an optional link to the full filing, and the
fine-print forward-looking-statements disclaimer that a report of record
requires. The rhythm alternates paper and navy, sparse and dense, voice and
figure, so the eye keeps moving through what is, by nature, a long document.
