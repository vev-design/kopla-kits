# Pitch Deck

A scroll-driven presentation system for pitch decks, keynotes, and investor
stories. The default scheme is bold and dark — near-black canvas, near-white
ink, and a single electric cyan accent used sparingly for emphasis. Type runs
oversized and confident: each section is a full-viewport slide-like beat with
one dominant idea, surrounded by quiet supporting elements. The motion language
is scroll-first — sections reveal as they enter the viewport, and the hero stat
scales with scroll progress so the deck reads as a moving narrative rather than
a stack of static blocks.

Use this system when the goal is to persuade in a single linear scroll: a
founder pitch, a vision keynote, a fundraising story, a product launch
narrative. It is deliberately spare on web-app chrome (no toolbar, no footer) —
the viewer is meant to move through it top to bottom, one slide at a time.

## Composition

The order is a told story. **TitleSlide** opens cold on a full screen — the
deck's name, a one-line premise, and who is presenting — setting the bold, dark
tone before any content lands. **AgendaList** immediately frames the journey
with big numbered beats so the viewer knows the shape of what's coming. From
there the deck alternates dense and sparse: **StatBig** is the first hard
punch, a single oversized metric that scales as you scroll into it — the number
is the hero and earns a full screen alone. **ImageFull** then opens up into a
full-bleed visual moment with an overlaid line, giving the eye a wide, cinematic
breath after the intensity of the stat. **QuoteSlide** slows the pace to an
intimate, type-led beat — a customer or founder voice that carries credibility
in human terms. **ClosingCTA** lands the ask: a final statement, a single
call-to-action, and a contact line, closing the loop the TitleSlide opened. The
rhythm — cold open, map, punch, breath, voice, ask — is the design.
